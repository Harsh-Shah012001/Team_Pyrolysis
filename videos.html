Circular Economy for Plastics

Description

Once the plastic is produced and sent for commercial use, there is a huge amount of waste plastic generated. Out of the waste, only 35% of plastic is recycled. The rest 65% un-recycled plastic is dumped in landfills and incinerators. This results in soil as well as water production. If this, 65% plastic is processed through pyrolysis, it will create fuels as well as electricity. This will result in a circular economy and help our environment to get rid of plastic waste.


Circular Economy for Plastics

Description

Plastic recycling is the reprocessing of plastic waste into new and useful products. When performed correctly, this can reduce dependence on landfill, conserve resources and protect the environment from plastic pollution and greenhouse gas emissions. Recycling is necessary because almost all plastic is non-biodegradable and thus builds-up in the environment, where it can cause harm. Presently, almost all recycling is perform by remelting and reforming used plastic into new items; so-called mechanical recycling. This can cause polymer degradation at a chemical level, and also requires that waste be sorted by both colour and polymer type before being reprocessed..


Oil from Plastic Trash

Description

Today, plastics are used in every aspect of our daily lives and are inevitable for human beings. They are very convenient to use, but eventually when they become waste serious environmental problems arise. Most of the plastic is dumped in landfills in less than 6 months of its production. The burning and land filling of plastic waste results in ground water contamination and air pollution. The only solution to prevent this is recycling plastic. This is achieved through pyrolysis. In pyrolysis, the plastic waste is liquefied and then converted to fuel. This prevents air as well as water pollution. To know more about pyrolysis in detail, please watch the video.


How Waste Plastic is Converted into Fuel

Description

We can convert Plastic back into usable Fuel. This Process is Called " PLASTIC PYROLYSIS " Also Known as DEPOLYMERIZATION. In this process long Polymer molecules (plastic) are broken down into shorter chains of Hydrocarbons with the help of heat and pressure. A catalyst is added to lower the temperature and increase the yield.
